– m, n and h are the gating variables and they are dynamically varying with the voltage.

In the Hodgkin-Huxley model, one of the most famous models of neuronal behavior, there are three such gates, associated with sodium and potassium channels:

The m gate: This is the activation gate for the sodium channel. It controls the opening of the channel. The opening of the m gate is a fast process relative to the other gates.

The h gate: This is the inactivation gate for the sodium channel. It controls the closing of the channel, or the inactivation of the channel after it has opened. The closing of the h gate is a slower process than the opening of the m gate.

The n gate: This is the activation gate for the potassium channel. It controls the opening of the channel. The opening of the n gate is a slower process than the closing of the h gate.

The rates of these transitions are described by time constants. These time constants are usually functions of voltage and are typically fit empirically to experimental data. For the m, h, and n gates in the Hodgkin-Huxley model, these time constants generally have the following order: tau_m (m gate) < tau_h (h gate) < tau_n (n gate), meaning that the m gate is the fastest and the n gate is the slowest

Fixed points, or equilibrium points, show up as points where the vectors are zero (i.e., no arrows). Existence and Uniqueness Theorem:-

The Existence and Uniqueness Theorem is a fundamental result in the field of differential equations. It addresses whether solutions to certain types of problems exist and whether these solutions are unique.

Nullclines

The behavior of the phase plane is controlled by the isoclines or nullclines. These are the locus of points where the time derivatives of the state variables are zero, dV/dt=0 and dw/dt=0 for the MLE.

The V-nullcline is the set of points (V, w) in the phase plane where dV/dt = 0. Along this curve, the membrane potential V does not change; only the recovery variable w changes. This nullcline can be calculated by setting the right-hand side of the first differential equation to zero and solving for W as a function of V.

The w-nullcline is the set of points (V, w) in the phase plane where dw/dt = 0. Along this curve, the recovery variable w does not change; only the membrane potential V changes. This nullcline can be calculated by setting the right-hand side of the second differential equation to zero and solving for w as a function of V.

The intersection of the V and w nullclines corresponds to the fixed point or points of the system, where neither variable changes.
